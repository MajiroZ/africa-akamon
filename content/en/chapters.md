## National Chapters

Africa Akamon-kai is a continental organization that coordinates national chapters across Africa. Each chapter operates with substantial autonomy to address the specific needs of its country while maintaining coordination with the continental organization.

---

### Established Chapters

#### 🇰🇪 Kenya Chapter *(Founding Chapter)*

The Kenya Chapter serves as the founding chapter of Africa Akamon-kai and supports the establishment of additional national chapters across Africa. The Secretariat is currently based in Kenya.

---

### Tokyo Chapter

Africa Akamon-kai may also maintain a **Tokyo Chapter** to promote exchange, consultation, information sharing, and other support activities especially for African students, researchers, and other persons enrolled at or engaged with the University of Tokyo, together with graduates and other supporters.

---

### Start a Chapter in Your Country

We actively encourage the formation of new national chapters. The minimum requirements to establish a new chapter are:

1. At least **3 eligible members** (as defined in Article IV, Section 1) residing in or closely associated with the country who are committed to organizing the chapter
2. A demonstrated commitment to organizing activities and maintaining communication with the Continental Board
3. Confirmation of at least **1 person** willing to serve as the inaugural Chapter Leader or Coordinator

*The Continental Board may waive or modify these requirements to encourage network-building in countries with very small numbers of alumni.*

### Application Process

Interested alumni should submit a proposal to the Continental Board that outlines:

- A list of founding members with their University of Tokyo affiliations
- Proposed initial leadership (chapter leader or coordinator)
- Preliminary ideas for chapter activities
- A plan for communication with Africa Akamon-kai

New chapters must be approved by a majority of the Continental Board and subsequently by the University of Tokyo.

### Informal Groups

In countries where the formal minimum requirements are not yet met, alumni are encouraged to form informal groups or networks. These groups can organize occasional meetings, maintain contact via social media or messaging apps, and work toward eventual formal chapter status.
