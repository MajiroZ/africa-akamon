## About Africa Akamon-kai

**Africa Akamon-kai** (東京大学アフリカ同窓会 – 赤門会) is the official Pan-African Alumni Association of The University of Tokyo. We are a non-profit organization that operates exclusively to advance the educational mission of the University.

### Purpose

We promote mutual communication and support between The University of Tokyo and its alumni across the African continent, and encourage alumni association activities throughout Africa. Specifically, we contribute to the University of Tokyo community and Africa's development by:

- Facilitating the sharing of knowledge, expertise, and experience among alumni
- Providing support to current students and early-career researchers
- Enhancing the visibility of University of Tokyo research and educational activities in Africa
- Serving as a bridge between Japan and Africa in academic, business, and social spheres
- Promoting collaboration and partnership opportunities among alumni, students, and institutions in both regions
- Nurturing innovation and entrepreneurship within the alumni community
- Strengthening connections between the University of Tokyo and African academic and professional institutions

### Structure

Africa Akamon-kai operates as a continental organization encompassing national chapters across Africa. Each national chapter operates with autonomy within its country while maintaining coordination with Africa Akamon-kai on continent-wide initiatives.

**Founding Chapter:** Kenya Chapter serves as the founding chapter of Africa Akamon-kai.

### Continental Board

The Continental Board governs the affairs of Africa Akamon-kai across Africa. It includes:

- **President** (2-year term)
- **3 Vice Presidents** (1-year terms)
- **Immediate Past President** and **3 Immediate Past Vice Presidents**
- Up to 7 additional elected board members (3-year terms)
- One representative from each established national chapter (ex-officio)

Our full bylaws are available upon request. They govern membership, elections, chapter formation, and all other organizational matters.
