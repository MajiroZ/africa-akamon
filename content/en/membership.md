## Membership

Africa Akamon-kai welcomes University of Tokyo alumni across Africa. There are three membership categories:

---

### A. Regular Members

Persons who have been awarded a degree by the University of Tokyo.

### B. Associate Members

Persons who were enrolled at the University of Tokyo but did not receive a degree — including research students, postdoctoral fellows, visiting researchers, and exchange students — as well as persons awarded a certificate of completion from an official University of Tokyo program officially listed as providing "alumni eligibility."

Associate members have all the rights and privileges of regular members, except that they are not eligible to be elected as officers of the Continental Board.

### C. Honorary Members

- Persons awarded honorary degrees by the University of Tokyo
- Distinguished individuals nominated by the Board of Africa Akamon-kai and further approved by the University of Tokyo
- Outstanding individuals who have made significant contributions to strengthening the ties between the University of Tokyo and Africa

Honorary members have all the rights and privileges of regular members, except that they are not eligible to be elected as officers of the Continental Board.

---

## How to Apply

To join as a Regular, Associate, or Honorary member, submit an application to the Secretariat with:

1. A completed application form (including your University of Tokyo affiliation details)
2. Supporting documents as required

The Secretariat will review all applications and submit recommendations to the Continental Board (or Executive Committee) for final decision.

> **Note:** Honorary membership is not applied for. Any member may recommend a person for honorary membership to the Board by presenting appropriate details and justification.

---

## Chapter Affiliation

Members may affiliate with one or more national chapters based on their country of residence, nationality, country of origin, or professional involvement in Africa.
